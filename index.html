'Eye Of The Behodler' 2023
Anon
Polyjet resin 3D print with inserted Casascius physical Bitcoin (13DVQQ9i)



ABOUT

'Eye Of The Behodler' is a new work from an international artist, who for this project, reserves the right to anonymity.

Supported and facilitated by Utrophia, the piece has drawn from the talents of many artists from around the world, all working under the strict remit of the key artist.

The piece is a 3D polyjet resin print of an AI generated face, modelled to be ambiguous in terms of race and gender.

A fully loaded physical Casascius Bitcoin (13DVQQ9i) is slotted into the forehead of the piece



It comments on the deep state of meditation of the 'hodler', someone who holds Bitcoin and waits, transfixed by the phantasm of online information.

More broadly, it speaks of the tension between digital and anologue realities in our current economic, scientific and cultural situation.

Society has to evolve around the advent of new technology and with the current advancements we're seeing, our realities are becoming blurred.

Does that which comes from the digital realm resonate the same in the physical realm?



Economically the piece holds a base value of one Bitcoin plus the collectable value given the rarity of this early, brass, Casascius coin, many of which were redeemed since they were first issued in 2011.

Bitcoin has been positioned by its proponants as a digital store of value because of its programmed scarcity. It's presented as a 'digital gold'. Indeed, gold, land and art have been traditionally been stores of value in times of turbulence and at this point in time we are yet to see the consequences of the huge monetary experiment of QE.



We as humans speculate and adapt. We imagine how the world can be different, both for the better and for the worse. We plan and envisage. The digital age has elevated our forrays into these realms whereby when we are presented with base reality we don't know what to do with it.

Process

AI

The initial step was creating an AI generated face, eyes closed, shaved head, ambiguous in terms of race and gender.
Placed on the forehead is inserted the image of a physical Casascius Bitcoin. It evokes the 'third eye' of Hinduism, Buddhism, Taoism and Theosophy.

3D Modelling

Using Blender's 'Face Builder' extension, the 2D face was plotted and sculpted into a 3D model.
The face was built over the course of a year and had a team of artists based all around the UK working on it, coordinated by the project lead at Utrophia.

3D Printing

The print took three days and was then worked on by a group of artists to bring the finished article to a fine balance between smooth marbled detail and still retaining some of the lines of the 3D printing process.
